Choose your horizon.
Daylight
Daylight is the period of time when the sun is above the horizon and the sky is bright due to the sun's direct rays illuminating the Earth's atmosphere.
Night
Night is the period of time when the sun is below the horizon and the sky is dark, making stars and the moon become visible.
Twilight
Twilight is a period of time between day and night when the sun is below the horizon but its light is still visible in the sky, creating a dim and diffused illumination.
Civil
  • Brightest type of twilight
  • Occurs when the sun is between 0 and 6 degrees below the horizon
  • Enough light for most outdoor activities to be carried out without artificial lighting
Nautical
  • Dimmer than civil twilight
  • Occurs when the sun is between 6 and 12 degrees below the horizon
  • Difficult to distinguish the horizon from the sky, and navigation by the stars becomes possible
Astronomical
  • Darkest type of twilight
  • Occurs when the sun is between 12 and 18 degrees below the horizon
  • The sky is dark enough for astronomical observations to be carried out
